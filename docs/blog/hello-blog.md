# Why I Started This Blog

The documentation on the official site tells you *how to use this feature* — precise, clean, and complete. But a lot of things that come up in the work aren't quite tutorials, yet still belong to the whole system: a design trade-off, a workflow that took forever to figure out, or the story behind why something is named the way it is.

These feel too heavy for the docs, but too good to throw away. So this blog exists.

## How It's Different from the Official Site

The official site is dark and "product-launch" styled: indigo on near-black, restrained and sharp. I deliberately chose something different for the blog:

- A **warm, neutral dark gray** base instead of harsh pure black;
- The accent shifted from indigo to **sage green**, more relaxed and calming;
- Wider line spacing and a narrower measure, so long reads feel easier.

> In short: the official site is there to *convince* you; the blog is there to *keep you company* while you read.

## What I'll Write About

Mostly these:

1. The design thinking behind features;
2. Workflow fragments that don't quite fit a tutorial;
3. Occasional essays.

I'll write when something comes to mind and let it accumulate slowly. No update schedule — the goal is that, years from now, there's something worth coming back to.
